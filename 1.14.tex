The orders of growth for the count-change program are $ \Theta(n^5) $ because for every time the kinds-of-coins variable is be incremented, there will be approximately just as many new steps for every step that already existed. So if the count-change program had consisted of only three types of change, the order of growth would have been $ \Theta(n^3) $. $ \Theta(n^5) $ means that $ \Omega(n^5) $ and also $ \mathcal{O}(n^5) $.
